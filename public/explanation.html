Purpose

The purpose of this site is to:

  • Be a good way to read Revelation
  • Make it easy to focus on related sections of the book
  • Educate about the structure of Revelation

The overall pattern of Revelation

The book of Revelation is written in a symmetrical A, B, C, B, A pattern. This pattern is common to Hebrew literature. It is often known as a "chiasm" structure.

The septets that make up each section

Other than the prologue and epilogue, each section of the chiasm is comprised of an introduction, and a "septet" (7 parts).

Wilbur Pickering

This English translation of The Revelation of Jesus Christ is based on Wilbur Pickering's New Translation based on the Majority Text.

Some changes have been made to his revised translation - for more details, and a list of the changes, see this repository on Github.

The Greek translation is Pickering's collation of the majority (Byzantine) text.

Phillip Kayser

This chiastic outline was identified by Phil Kayser. It builds upon insights from over 20 scholars who see the book as following a chiastic structure but have been puzzled by the position of chapter 12 and have not been able to agree on the relationship of apparently disparate features (the introductions to the septets and the interludes).

Phil Kayser is the senior pastor at Dominion Covenant Church.
