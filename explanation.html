The overall pattern

The book of Revelation is written in a symmetrical A, B, C, B, A pattern. This pattern is common to Hebrew literature. It is often known as a "chiasm" structure.

The septets that make up each section

Other than the prologue and epilogue, each section of the chiasm is comprised of an introduction, and a "septet" (7 parts).

This chiastic outline was identified by Phil Kayser. It builds upon insights from over 20 scholars who see the book as following a chiastic structure but who have not been able to agree on 1) the relationship of apparently disparate features (highlighted above in blue and yellow) and 2) who are puzzled by the position of chapter 12.
